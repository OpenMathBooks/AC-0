The functions \(f(x) = x^{10}\) and \(g(x) = 2^x\) intersect at three points: \((-0.94, 0.52)\text{,}\) \((1.08, 2.11)\text{,}\) and another point near \(x \approx 60\text{.}\) The \(y\) values on both functions at the third point are very very large, but at this point the function \(g(x) = 2^x\) dominates the power function \(f(x) = x^{10}\text{.}\)
