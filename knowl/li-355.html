When considering the functions \(f(x) = e^x\) and \(g(x) = x^{10}\) we know that the exponential function will dominate over the power function based on our work in prior activities. Therefore, \(h(x) = f(x)/g(x) \to \infty\) as \(x\to\infty\) and \(k(x) = g(x)/f(x) \to 0\) as \(x \to \infty\text{.}\)
