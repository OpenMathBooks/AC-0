This function clearly has three roots but the end behavior indicates an even degree polynomial. The root at \(x=1\) is special since we do not cross the \(x\) axis. Hence, the factors are \((x+1)\text{,}\) \((x-1)\text{,}\) \((x-1)\text{,}\) and \((x-2)\text{.}\) Notice that the \((x-1)\) root is listed twice, and hence the polynomial takes the form \(c(x) = C(x+1)(x-1)^2(x-2)\text{.}\) The point \((0,-2)\) appears to be on the graph so \(C = 1\) and the polynomial is

\begin{equation*} c(x) = (x+1)(x-1)^2(x-2). \end{equation*} in-context
