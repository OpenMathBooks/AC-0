This function clearly has three roots and the ends show the opposite behavior. Since the right-hand side of the function tends down we expect a negative leading coefficient. Based on the roots \(x=-2\text{,}\) \(x=1\text{,}\) and \(x=3\) the factors are \((x+2)\text{,}\) \((x-1)\text{,}\) and \((x-3)\) and the polynomial takes the form \(b(x) = C(x+2)(x-1)(x-3)\text{.}\) The point \((-1,-8)\) appears to be on the graph so \(C = -1\) and the polynomial is

\begin{equation*} b(x) = -(x+2)(x-1)(x-3). \end{equation*}
