A function \(f\) has a horizontal asymptote \(y = a\) if the distance between the \(f\) and the line \(y=a\) becomes arbitrarily small when \(x\) becomes sufficiently large. Alternatively, a horizontal asymptote is a line that a function approaches as \(x \to \infty\) or as \(x \to -\infty\text{.}\)
