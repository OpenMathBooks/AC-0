For odd values of \(n\text{,}\) the graphs of \(x^{n}\) are always increasing. For even values of \(n\text{,}\) the graphs of \(x^{n}\) decrease until \(x=0\) and then increase afterwards. See FigureÂ 3 for several examples of power functions. We can classify the end behavior of the graphs by describing what happens as \(x\to\infty\) and as \(x\to-\infty\text{.}\) For odd \(n\text{,}\) \(x^{n}\to -\infty\) as \(x\to-\infty\) and \(x^{n}\to\infty\) as \(x\to\infty\text{.}\) Graphs of odd power functions go in opposite directions on the left and right. For even \(n\text{,}\) \(x^{n}\to \infty\) as \(x\to-\infty\) and \(x^{n}\to\infty\) as \(x\to\infty\text{.}\) Graphs of even power functions go in the same direction on the left and right.
